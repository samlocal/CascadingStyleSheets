Nallamala Forest, located in southern India, is renowned for its rich biodiversity and lush landscapes. This dense forest area, part of the Eastern Ghats, is a critical habitat for various wildlife species, including the elusive Bengal tiger. The Nallamala region, with its rugged terrain and extensive vegetation, provides an ideal environment for these apex predators. Conservation efforts in the forest focus on protecting tiger populations and their habitat, mitigating human-wildlife conflict, and preserving the ecosystem's ecological balance. The presence of tigers in Nallamala underscores the forest's importance as a sanctuary for wildlife and highlights ongoing conservation challenges.
